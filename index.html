Real-Life Bully Scenarios

Each of the 100 scenarios presents a real situation, 3 response choices, and a reflection on consequences — teaching critical thinking, empathy, and moral courage.

📱 Online
👊 Physical

Scenario 1 · School
A classmate is mocked about their school uniform.
Laughing with the bully strengthens bad behaviour. Quietly supporting the victim or reporting to an adult is most effective.
Classroom
Scenario 3 · School
A student finds their bag hidden by a group of classmates.
Staying silent makes you complicit. Speaking up or getting a teacher prevents escalation.
Classroom
Scenario 7 · School
A student is consistently left out during group projects.
Exclusion is social bullying. Include others — being kind costs nothing but means everything.
Group Work
Scenario 12 · School
A new student is called hurtful names at the canteen.
Walking away is not enough. A kind word or sitting beside the new student changes everything.
Canteen
Scenario 18 · School
Someone copies an exam answer and blames the seat-mate.
Courage to speak the truth protects the innocent and builds trust with teachers.
Exam Hall
Scenario 22 · School
A student's artwork is destroyed by a jealous classmate.
Report calmly and with evidence. Anger escalates; calm reporting resolves.
Art Class
Scenario 30 · Online
A student's photo is shared in a class group chat without permission.
Never share someone else's image without consent. Screenshot and report to a trusted adult immediately.
WhatsApp
Scenario 35 · Online
Someone creates a fake social media account using a friend's name and photos.
This is identity theft and cyberbullying. Report to the platform AND involve parents or teachers.
Social Media
Scenario 41 · Online
A gaming group excludes and mocks a player for losing.
Online spaces must have the same kindness as real life. Defend your teammate — it sets the tone for the whole group.
Online Gaming
Scenario 50 · Online
Hurtful comments flood a student's social media post.
Don't engage with negative commenters. Screenshot, block, and tell a trusted adult. Use the EGK Anti-Bully Careline.
Social Media
Scenario 58 · Online
A student is threatened via private message to keep a secret or face consequences.
This is digital coercion. Never keep such secrets — tell a trusted adult immediately and use the careline.
Messaging App
Scenario 65 · Online
A rumour about a classmate spreads rapidly through multiple group chats.
Stop the chain — don't forward. Speak the truth to those around you. Rumours shrink when people refuse to share them.
Group Chat
Scenario 5 · Physical
A bigger student blocks the corridor and demands money from younger students.
Walk away safely, find another route, then report to a teacher or use the careline. Never confront alone.
Corridor
Scenario 9 · Physical
A student's book is snatched and thrown across the field.
Safety first. Witness it? Calmly report. Victim? Tell a teacher and document what happened.
Field
Scenario 15 · Physical
During PE, a student is deliberately pushed and injured.
Physical bullying is a serious offence. Seek medical attention first, then report formally — use the PDRMBorder app if needed.
PE Class
Scenario 80 · Community
Younger children are bullied at a community playground by older teens.
Contact a nearby adult, call a parent, or use the PDRMBorder app to find the nearest police station quickly.
Public Space
Scenario 20 · Social
A student is deliberately left out of a birthday invitation while the party is discussed loudly in front of them.
Exclusion is emotional bullying. If you see it happening, privately check in on the excluded person — your small kindness is enormous to them.
Social Exclusion
Scenario 33 · Social
A student from a different race is mocked about their food during lunch.
Cultural sensitivity starts at the lunch table. Defend your friend's right to their culture.
Cultural Bully
Scenario 47 · Social
Rumours about a student's family background are whispered and spread in the class.
Rumours destroy reputations. Refuse to participate. Kindly correct misinformation when you hear it.
Rumour
Scenario 72 · Social
A student with a disability is consistently mimicked and laughed at.
Everyone deserves dignity. Stand beside them publicly. Your support signals to the bully that this community does not accept cruelty.
Disability Bias
Core Principles

Three Pillars of Safe Response

Every scenario in the book is built on three guiding principles — empowering students, teachers, and parents to act wisely and safely.

🛡️
Safety First
Always prioritise personal safety. Never confront a bully alone or in an unsafe situation. Remove yourself first, then report from a safe position.
🫂
Meaningful Support
Being a friend to the victim makes the biggest difference. A kind word, sitting beside someone, or privately checking in can change a life.
📢
Report Correctly
Involve trusted adults or authorities safely. Use the EGK Anti-Bully Careline App, the PDRMBorder App, or speak to a teacher — document, don't delete evidence.

About the Author

One Decision to Care

"Keberanian untuk Peduli was written to invite readers to think, not to punish; to understand, not to point fingers. Every scenario in this book reflects a real situation that can happen in schools — in classrooms, on the field, in peer groups, and in the digital world.
