Why Malaysia

A Nation in Motion

Malaysia is investing billions in world-class public transport — from urban rail in Kuala Lumpur to the game-changing ECRL linking the East Coast to world ports. EGK's apps put every route, timetable, and connection in your pocket.

🚄
East Coast Rail Link (ECRL)
665km mega-rail from Kota Bharu to Westports — connecting Malaysia's East and West Coasts like never before.
🤖
Autonomous Rail Transit (ART)
Futuristic trackless ART systems in Putrajaya and Kuching — Southeast Asia's most advanced urban mobility.
⛴️
Penang — Pearl of the Orient
The iconic Penang Ferry, future LRT Mutiara, and George Town's UNESCO World Heritage streets await every visitor.
🌿
Rainforests & Culture
From the world's largest flower Rafflesia to the grandeur of Perdana Putra — Malaysia is endlessly extraordinary.

Tips for Exploring Malaysia

Get the most out of Malaysia's world-class transport with these insider tips from EGK.

📲
Download Before You Go
All EGK apps work offline — download your routes at the hotel before heading out. No roaming data needed for timetables!
🎫
Touch & Go is King
Malaysia's Touch 'n Go card works on MRT, LRT, KTM, and even Penang Ferry. Tap in, tap out — zero queuing.
⛴️
Penang: Take the Ferry First
Don't drive to Penang Island. Park at Butterworth, take the ferry, and explore George Town on foot or by rickshaw.
🌙
Off-Peak Travel
KL trains get busy 7–9am and 5–7pm. Plan ECRL trips mid-morning for the best views of the East Coast jungle corridor.
🗺️
Putrajaya by ART
Malaysia's federal capital is stunning. The ART system connects government buildings, gardens, and lakeside attractions seamlessly.
🌿
Kuching by ART
Sarawak's capital is one of Asia's most liveable cities. The upcoming KUTS ART will transform how visitors explore Kuching.
